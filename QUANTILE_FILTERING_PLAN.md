# Quantile Range Filtering Strategy Change: [0.9, 1] → [0.8, 1]

This plan outlines the changes required to refocus the causal-targeting pipeline from the current [0.9, 1] quantile range (top 10%) to the [0.8, 1] quantile range (top 20%) for subgroup identification and validation.

## Background

The current pipeline identifies high-treatment-effect subgroups using:
- **Validation quantile:** `q* = 0.9` (top 10% of predicted effects)
- **Subgroup thresholds:** `q_bot=0.9, q_top=1.0`

The proposed change shifts to:
- **Validation quantile:** `q* = 0.8` (top 20% of predicted effects)
- **Subgroup thresholds:** `q_bot=0.8, q_top=1.0`

This wider quantile range may improve:
1. Statistical power (larger subgroups)
2. Robustness of treatment effect estimates
3. Practical applicability of findings

## Metric Definitions (from StaDISC)

### Indicator B (Top Quantile Dominance)

For each (estimator, CV, fold, quantile q):
- **B_q = 1** if τ̂_top_q ≥ τ̂_complement (top subgroup outperforms complement)
- **B_q = 0** otherwise

Aggregation:
- **B̄_q = mean(B_q across all 12 folds)** — proportion of folds where indicator = 1

### Treatment Effect Difference Δτ

- **Δτ_q = τ̂_top_q − τ̂_complement** for each fold
- **Δτ̄_q = mean(Δτ_q across 12 folds)**

---

## Ensemble Selection Criteria

Using `B̄_0.8` and `Δτ̄_0.8`:

**Selection Procedure:**
1. **Primary:** Maximize `B̄_0.8` (proportion of folds where top 20% outperforms complement)
2. **Tiebreaker:** Largest `Δτ̄_0.8` among configurations with highest B̄_0.8
